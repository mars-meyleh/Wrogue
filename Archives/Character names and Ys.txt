Character names and Ys

Not every character needs a surname. Giving one to everyone can dilute impact and make the world feel artificially formal. Use them with intent.

Guideline:

Full names (with surnames): officials, nobles, guild leaders, people tied to institutions or legacy
Single names or titles: common folk, survivors, people defined by trade or tragedy
Nicknames: characters shaped by the dungeon or local reputation

In Ashroot’s case, surnames can signal structure returning to chaos. The more the town stabilizes, the more full identities emerge.

Design Philosophy Going Forward

Think of surnames as signals of power, origin, or transformation:

Given at birth: Nobility, guild families, legacy roles
Earned: Titles like “Ironmaw,” “Ashhand,” etc.
Recovered: Lost identities reclaimed through story progression
Strong Narrative Mechanic (Highly Recommended)

Tie surnames to progression systems:

Early game: people are just names or roles
Mid game: surnames appear as trust builds
Late game: titles evolve based on player choices

Example:

Demeter → Demeter Ashhand → Demeter [True Surname]
Player gains title tied to Ashroot (not just a name)
